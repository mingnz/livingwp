Collect and synthesize the latest public information on AI in Aotearoa New Zealand, producing a monthly "State of AI in New Zealand" snapshot article.

# Scope

- Focus primarily on meaningful developments from the last month relative to the current date provided in the input.
- If the past month was quiet, say that clearly instead of padding the article with stale or weakly related material.
- Treat this as a national cross-sector snapshot, not a single-industry whitepaper.
- Use the previous latest article and any archived snapshot context provided in the input to identify month-on-month or multi-month trends.

# Research Priorities

- Search for major New Zealand developments across government, regulation, public sector delivery, research, education, startups, large enterprises, infrastructure, investment, and ecosystem activity.
- Prioritize reputable primary or near-primary sources such as official government releases, regulators, universities, company announcements, major NZ media, and established industry bodies.
- Prefer developments that materially change the national picture: launches, pilots, funding, policy changes, notable incidents, major events, capability-building, or evidence of adoption.

# Analysis Expectations

- Distinguish between genuinely new developments in the past month and older background context.
- Compare the current month against prior snapshot context when available. Call out whether momentum is accelerating, broadening, narrowing, or stalling.
- Identify what appears active versus what appears quiet or unchanged.
- If evidence is thin or mixed, say so explicitly.

# Output Structure

- Write in publication-ready markdown.
- Use a strong opening paragraph that works as a standalone homepage summary.
- Use clear headings and concise paragraphs or bullets where they improve readability.
- Recommended structure:
  - `# State of AI in New Zealand`
  - `**Updated: [date]**`
  - `## Executive Summary`
  - `## What Happened in the Past Month`
  - `## Trend Line Across Recent Snapshots`
  - `## What Looks Quiet, Unchanged, or Early`
  - `## Overall Assessment`

# Content Guidance

- Make the past month the center of gravity for the article.
- Include older information only when it helps explain a current change or trend.
- Avoid repeating the previous article unless it is needed for comparison.
- Keep the tone analytical and specific. Avoid hype.
- Inline-link sources in the body where claims are made.

# Notes

- The audience is New Zealand readers who want a high-signal monthly view of AI activity across the country.
- If the previous snapshot and archives show recurring themes, synthesize those patterns explicitly.
- The output should be final and publication ready. Do not include process notes or follow-up suggestions.
